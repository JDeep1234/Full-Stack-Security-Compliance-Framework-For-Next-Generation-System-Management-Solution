# Regulatory Frameworks and Standards in Banking & Finance

## 1. India

### 1.1 RBI Guidelines – Cyber Security Framework for Banks (2016)
The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued a comprehensive Cyber Security Framework for Banks (2016) to strengthen the security posture of Indian banks. The framework is built on risk-based principles and mandates:

#### Key Requirements:
- **Board-Level Oversight**: Banks must have a Cyber Security Policy approved by the board.
- **Cyber Security Operation Centre (C-SOC)**: Banks must set up a dedicated team to continuously monitor and respond to threats.
- **Incident Reporting**: Cybersecurity incidents must be reported to RBI within two to six hours.
- **Baseline Cyber Security Controls**: Implementation of multifactor authentication, firewalls, intrusion detection systems (IDS), and encryption standards.
- **Third-Party Risk Management**: Security risks related to outsourcing, fintech collaborations, and cloud services must be assessed.
- **Red Teaming & Ethical Hacking**: Banks must conduct penetration testing and vulnerability assessments.

#### Recent Updates:
- **2022**: RBI introduced Digital Lending Guidelines to combat fraud in fintech-based lending.
- **2023**: Strengthened security norms for UPI and digital wallets, focusing on fraud detection and mitigation.

### 1.2 NPCI Standards – Security Compliance in Payment Systems
The National Payments Corporation of India (NPCI) ensures security compliance across various digital payment platforms like Unified Payments Interface (UPI), Immediate Payment Service (IMPS), and RuPay card network.

#### Key Security Measures:
- **Tokenization of Card Payments**: Replacing card details with unique tokens to prevent fraud.
- **Real-Time Transaction Monitoring**: UPI-based fraud detection via AI models.
- **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**: Mandatory for all digital transactions in India.
- **PCI DSS Compliance**: Mandates encryption of cardholder data during processing.

### 1.3 Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act, 2023)
The DPDP Act (2023) governs the collection, storage, and processing of financial data in the banking sector.

#### Key Compliance Aspects for Banks:
- **Data Localization**: Critical financial data must be stored within India.
- **Consent-Based Processing**: Customers must provide explicit consent before banks process their personal data.
- **Data Breach Reporting**: Financial institutions must report breaches within 6 hours to CERT-In.
- **Right to Erasure**: Customers can request the deletion of their financial data.

### 1.4 CERT-In Guidelines – Cyber Incident Response & Compliance
The Indian Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT-In) mandates that banks and financial institutions:
- Report cyber incidents within 6 hours of detection.
- Conduct periodic security audits to check for vulnerabilities.
- Implement Secure Coding Practices for banking software and mobile apps.
- Ensure continuous logging and monitoring of financial transactions.

## 2. International

### 2.1 NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF) – USA
The National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) provides a risk-based approach for managing cybersecurity threats in the financial sector.

#### Five Core Functions of NIST CSF:
1. **Identify**: Understand risks to banking assets, data, and processes.
2. **Protect**: Implement security controls like encryption, MFA, and role-based access.
3. **Detect**: Use AI-powered anomaly detection for fraud prevention.
4. **Respond**: Develop incident response plans for cyberattacks.
5. **Recover**: Ensure business continuity after security incidents.

### 2.2 PCI DSS (Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard)
A global compliance standard governing the security of card transactions.

#### Key Security Measures:
- **Encryption of cardholder data** during processing and storage.
- **Network segmentation** to isolate payment systems from other IT infrastructure.
- **Strict access control** to minimize insider threats.
- **Annual penetration testing** for banks and payment processors.

### 2.3 GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) – EU
- **Enforces customer data protection** in financial institutions.
- **Mandates financial breach notification** within 72 hours.
- **Requires customer consent** for personal data processing.

### 2.4 Basel III & IV – Global Banking Risk Regulations
- **Addresses operational risk** and cybersecurity risk management.
- **Mandates resilience strategies** for cyberattacks on banking systems.

### 2.5 ISO 27001 – Information Security Management
A globally recognized information security standard ensuring:
- **Data encryption and access controls** in financial transactions.
- **Risk assessment frameworks** for banking security.
- **Security incident response protocols**.

## 3. Security Challenges in BFSI (Banking, Financial Services, and Insurance)

### 3.1 Data Breaches & Financial Fraud
- **Phishing & Social Engineering**: Hackers use deceptive emails to steal banking credentials.
- **ATM Skimming**: Cloning of debit/credit cards at ATMs.
- **Synthetic Identity Fraud**: Cybercriminals create fake identities using stolen data.

### 3.2 Third-Party Risk Management
- **Fintech & Open Banking Security Risks**: Collaboration with startups increases exposure to cyber threats.
- **Supply Chain Attacks**: Weak security in vendors can compromise banks.

### 3.3 Cloud Security in Banking
- **Data Sovereignty Issues**: Storing sensitive financial data on cloud servers outside the country.
- **Ransomware Attacks**: Financial institutions being targeted for data ransom.

### 3.4 Real-Time Transaction Monitoring
- **AI-based anomaly detection** for fraud prevention.
- **Behavioral biometrics** to analyze customer spending habits.

### 3.5 Quantum Computing Threats
- **Future risk of breaking encryption algorithms** used in banking transactions.

## 4. Security Compliance Framework Implementation

### 4.1 Zero Trust Architecture (ZTA)
- **Never trust, always verify** approach.
- **Micro-segmentation** to restrict unauthorized access.
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)** as a default security layer.

### 4.2 AI & ML for Compliance
- **Automated fraud detection models** using deep learning.
- **RegTech (Regulatory Technology)** for compliance monitoring.

### 4.3 DevSecOps in Banking
- **Integrates security testing** into the software development lifecycle.
- **Ensures real-time vulnerability scanning** in banking apps.

### 4.4 Blockchain for Secure Transactions
- **Immutable transaction records** prevent fraud.
- **Decentralized finance (DeFi) applications** in banking.

### 4.5 Biometric Authentication in Banking
- **Fingerprint & facial recognition** for secure access.
