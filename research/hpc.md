
# HPC-Specific Compliance and Governance Frameworks 

## FAIR Data Principles in HPC

**Description:** FAIR principles (Findable, Accessible, Interoperable, Reusable) adapted specifically for the high-performance computing context, addressing the challenges of managing and sharing large-scale computational research outputs.

**Detailed aspects:**
- **Computational Workflow Reproducibility:** Requirements for documenting computational environments, software versions, and execution parameters
- **Large Dataset Management:** Protocols for handling multi-terabyte or petabyte datasets generated by HPC simulations
- **Metadata Standards:** HPC-specific metadata schemas capturing computational methods, resource usage, and provenance
- **Data Transfer Mechanisms:** Secure, high-performance methods for moving large datasets between systems while maintaining integrity
- **Long-term Storage Planning:** Guidelines for preserving large computational results that may be impossible to regenerate
- **Performance Impact Considerations:** Approaches to implementing FAIR principles with minimal impact on computational performance
- **Versioning for Large Simulations:** Specialized versioning approaches for massive simulation outputs
- **Citation Standards:** Methods for properly attributing computational resources and methods in scientific publications

**Implementation:**
Implementation typically involves developing Data Management Plans (DMPs) specific to HPC projects, integrating metadata capture into computational workflows, and establishing project-specific data sharing protocols.

**Reference:** [GO FAIR Initiative](https://www.go-fair.org/fair-principles/) and [FAIR for Research Software](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41597-022-01710-x)

## DOE HPC Operational Assessment (OAR)

**Description:** The U.S. Department of Energy conducts rigorous operational assessments of its leadership computing facilities, establishing comprehensive standards for security, availability, and scientific productivity.

**Detailed aspects:**
- **System Availability Metrics:** Standardized measurements of system uptime, scheduled maintenance, and unplanned outages
- **Resource Utilization Efficiency:** Metrics for evaluating effective use of computational resources including CPU hours, node usage, and queue efficiency
- **Scientific Impact Assessment:** Frameworks for measuring scientific output, publications, and breakthrough science enabled by HPC resources
- **Security Posture Evaluation:** Comprehensive security assessments adapted to the unique requirements of supercomputing environments
- **User Support Effectiveness:** Standards for evaluating user support, training, and documentation
- **Data Center Efficiency:** Metrics for power usage effectiveness (PUE) and energy efficiency
- **Technology Innovation Assessment:** Evaluation of facility contributions to HPC technology advancement
- **Project Management Compliance:** Standards for project planning, execution, and reporting

**Implementation:**
Implementation involves continuous monitoring systems, regular reporting cycles, annual comprehensive assessments, and improvement plans addressing identified weaknesses.

**Reference:** [Oak Ridge Leadership Computing Facility Operational Assessment](https://www.olcf.ornl.gov/for-users/hpc-operational-assessment-opa/) and [Argonne Leadership Computing Facility Operational Assessment](https://publications.anl.gov/anlpubs/2020/12/167606.pdf)
